The Big Three

Firstly, I want to address the confusion in the Anime community as to which anime are the big three and why that is. The big three consists exclusively of One Piece, Naruto, and Bleach—and to be clear, this list is immutable. Any other list you may have seen as it relates to the topic is incorrect, and I'll explain why this is the case.

These three anime are considered the big three due to their massive popularity during the early 2000s, their long-running manga series, and their significant impact on the anime industry as a whole during their era. Each of these series has a dedicated fanbase and has contributed to the global spread of anime culture. While other anime like Solo Leveling and My Hero Academia are incredibly popular, they do not hold the same historical significance as the original big three.

Dragon Ball Z on the other hand is often mistakenly included in discussions about the big three, but it predates this era and is considered a separate entity in anime history. But don't worry, DBZ is still considered the grandfather of shonen anime and has paved the way for many series that followed (including the big three). The big three set the standard for shonen anime and paved the way for future series to gain international acclaim. In this article, I will discuss why these three anime deserve their spot as the big three and what makes them stand out in the anime world.

One Piece

One Piece, created by Eiichiro Oda, is a story about a group of pirates led by Monkey D. Luffy as they search for the legendary treasure known as "One Piece." The series is known for its unique characters, intricate world-building, and epic adventures. One Piece has been praised for its storytelling, character development, and its ability to balance humor and action. It has become one of the best-selling manga series of all time and has a massive global fanbase.

Naruto

Naruto, created by Masashi Kishimoto, follows the journey of Naruto Uzumaki, a young ninja with dreams of becoming the strongest ninja and leader of his village. The series explores themes of friendship, perseverance, and self-discovery. Naruto has been lauded for its complex characters, intense battles, and emotional storytelling. It has also spawned a successful sequel series, Naruto Shippuden, and has had a significant impact on popular culture worldwide.

Bleach

Bleach, created by Tite Kubo, centers around Ichigo Kurosaki, a teenager who gains the powers of a Soul Reaper and must protect the living world from evil spirits known as Hollows. The series is known for its stylish action sequences, unique character designs, and supernatural elements. Bleach has been praised for its imaginative world-building and dynamic fight scenes. It has also garnered a dedicated fanbase and has been adapted into an anime series, movies, and video games.

Together

The big three are all unique in their own way, but they share a common thread of excellence in storytelling, character development, and world-building. They have all contributed to the evolution of anime and have inspired countless creators and fans around the world. They held down the anime industry during their peak years and continue to be beloved by fans of all ages.
